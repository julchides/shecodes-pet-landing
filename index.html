K&A

Two cats, one story,
a little drama, a little comedy,
but first of all a lot of love!

Name Kishka
[ ' k'i ʃ k ʌ ]
Date of Birth February 23, 2012
Place of Birth Kherson, Ukraine
Breed Scottish Straight Shorthair
Behavior Calm, confident, wise, patient and forgiving
Prefers Watching the world from the height of her wisdom
Dislikes Disturbing her inner peace

The word 'kishka' in Ukrainian means 'cat'. It so happened that not a single name stuck, so it turned out that this is a cat named 'Cat'. We met her at the cat show in Nikolaev at the beginning of May 2012. A cute playful kitten chose us, and we left there with her. She captured our hearts from the very first moment. Therefore, Kishka is the main member of our family.

Kishka is always watching everything that happens and controls all processes. Never without a reason will give a voice and will not release her claws. She endures a lot, forgives a lot, so Kishka is the wisest member of our family.

Name Asia
[ ' ʌ s'ʌ ]
Date of Birth June 8, 2015
Place of Birth Mykolaiv, Ukraine
Breed Scottish Straight Shorthair
Behavior She pretends to be mischievous, but she is actually very affectionate
Prefers Anxiety about everything at the same time and express her dissatisfaction
Dislikes Public displays of affection

Asya is the daughter of Kishka. Kishka gave birth to only two litters, Asya from the second. Asya was the last to be born, and Kishka was so tired during childbirth that she no longer had the strength to gnaw the pouch and lick the kitten. Asya could die. But we tried to save her and affectionately poked Kishka's muzzle into a small wet ball. Kishka gathered all her strength and performed all the necessary actions, Asya survived.

But Asya's sorrows did not end there. Our friend took her and treated her well. Unfortunately, due to work, he had to leave the country and he temporarily left Asya in the care of his friend. When it turned out that due to work he needs to continue to live in another country, Asya had to be given to someone forever. So it happened that we took her back. At that time she was already two years old. Her character showed that our friend's acquaintance treated her very badly. She was afraid of people, was very aggressive and unfriendly.

But love conquers all! Now there is almost no trace of the aggressive, impudent hairball that constantly hid behind the sofa. She competes with Kishka for the championship in our family. She especially loves children as if they were her own.
